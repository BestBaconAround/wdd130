History

White water rafting is an adventurous outdoor activity that involves navigating a river in an inflatable raft. It usually takes place on rough, fast-moving waters with varying levels of rapids.

Rafters must work together to steer and paddle while avoiding obstacles like rocks and drops. The thrill of the rapids and the challenge of teamwork make it a popular choice for adrenaline seekers and nature lovers.
